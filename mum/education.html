on Education

Few things make me angrier than the idea that any child's education should be sacrificed to the ideology of "the greater good" that resulted in the proliferation of what Alastair Campbell allegedly called "bog standard comprehensives".

If you ask any of my opponents about educational standards I can guarantee you that they will say that they want all schools to be great. This is a debate avoidance technique that is no use to parents who have real young children and real educational choices to make.

There are too few places in Primary schools. On the doorstep, I hear far too often from anguished parents who were unable to get their 5 year olds into the nearest schools, often when they have moved house to be in the catchment area for their preferred schools.
