C a r o l i n e     A t t f i e l d
on Europe

When I ran to be an MEP in 2014, I was full of hope and reforming zeal. Our prime minister has promised to lead reform and change in Europe and boy did it need it. I found a bloated and inefficient organisation supporting large numbers of people with non-jobs, thousands of lobbyists pouring money into parties to buy influence in a non transparent way and don't get me started on the cost and waste of time of maintaining duplicate buildings in Strasbourg that are only occupied for a handful of days a year.

This is an organisation which:
  • Cannot produce a set of auditted accounts
  • Spends money on itself at an eye watering speed
  • Pays the Duke of Westminster- the richest Englishman in the country £637,709 in farm subsidies just because he owns so much land
  • Pays millions to Russian Oligarchs and Oil rich sheiks for the same reasons Click here to see how many rich and famous foreigners receive these payments
  • Tells me that I cannot put a ladder up against my house to clear my gutters under the EU health and safety directive
  • Tells us that if we want to trade with South Africa we have to abide by the 2,100 pages of a trade agreement whose main purpose is to protect the European wine trade from South African Chardonnay.
  • Puts a trade agreement with the US beyond delivery - the TTIP will never be signed there are too many vested interests amongst the 27

During the Referendum campaign no one on either side could find a good word to say about the EU as an Organisation.

I am excited and optimistic about the opportunites of a post Brexit world and although there may be bumps on the road I look forward to living in a country where our parliament controls our laws, our money and our borders.
