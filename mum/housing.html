on Housing

When politicians say that "we" need to build more housing what they mean but are too afraid to say is that house prices are too high. This dates all the way back to the Barker report, commissioned by the Blair government in 2003. The report identified the number of new properties needed to stop the rise in house prices. This was spun as Britain having "a structural undersupply of new housing" and this avoidance of the unpleasant truth continues to this day.

The truth is that London needs more affordable housing but what it is getting in Lewisham, Deptford, Greenwich and Woolwich is thousands of high-rise apartments that are clearly priced beyond the means of first-time buyers.
